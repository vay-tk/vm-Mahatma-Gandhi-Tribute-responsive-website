About Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India, was a prominent leader who advocated for non-violent civil disobedience in the struggle for Indian independence from British rule. His approach to social and political change inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world.

He played a crucial role in India's independence movement, leading various campaigns such as the Salt March in 1930, which was a direct action against British salt laws. His principles of non-violence (Ahimsa) and truth (Satyagraha) continue to resonate in social justice movements today.

Philosophy

Ahimsa (Non-violence)

Gandhi believed in non-violence as the means to achieve political and social goals. He argued that violence only begets more violence and that true strength lies in peaceful resistance.

Satyagraha (Truth-force)

This was his principle of nonviolent resistance to tyranny through mass civil disobedience. He encouraged people to stand firm in their belief in justice and truth.

Simple Living

Gandhi lived a life of simplicity, promoting self-sufficiency and the use of local resources. He believed in the importance of minimalism and sustainable living.

Legacy

Gandhi's philosophy and teachings continue to inspire movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. Leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela drew inspiration from his methods of non-violent protest. His influence extends beyond politics into various aspects of social reform, environmental issues, and personal development.

His birth anniversary, October 2, is celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti in India and as the International Day of Non-Violence globally, reminding us of the power of peace and non-violence in resolving conflicts.

Timeline of Key Events

1869

Born in Porbandar, India, on October 2.

1893

Gandhi first practices civil disobedience in South Africa against racial discrimination.

1915

Returns to India and begins to organize the rural population for civil rights.

1930

Leads the Salt March, a 240-mile march to protest British salt laws.

1942

Launches the Quit India Movement, demanding an end to British rule.

1947

India gains independence from British rule on August 15.

1948

Assassinated on January 30 by Nathuram Godse.

Influence on Modern Movements

Gandhi's teachings have significantly impacted various social movements worldwide, advocating for civil rights, peace, and justice. His approach to non-violent protest has been adopted by many leaders in the fight for equality and freedom.

In India, his principles guided the nation towards independence. Globally, his influence can be seen in movements such as the American civil rights movement, led by Martin Luther King Jr., and the anti-apartheid movement in South Africa, led by Nelson Mandela.
